Air fryer capacity is measured in quarts, and picking the right size mostly comes down to how many people you're regularly cooking for — not just how much you can afford to spend.

## Quick Sizing Guide

- **2–3 quarts**: Best for one person or occasional single-serving snacks.
- **4–5 quarts**: A solid fit for couples or small households cooking a few times a week.
- **6 quarts**: The sweet spot for most families of 3–5, large enough for a full basket of fries or several chicken thighs at once.
- **8+ quarts**: Best for larger households or anyone who wants to cook a whole chicken or larger batches in one pass.

## Other Factors Beyond Size

**Counter space.** Larger baskets mean a bigger unit overall — measure your available counter and cabinet storage space before buying.

**Basket shape.** Square baskets generally use available volume more efficiently than round ones, fitting more food per quart of rated capacity.

**Presets vs. manual control.** Digital presets are convenient for common foods, but manual time/temperature control gives more flexibility for recipes that don't fit a preset.
