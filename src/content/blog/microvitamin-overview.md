MicroVitamin markets itself around transparency in the supplement industry — publishing exact ingredient forms and dosages rather than using proprietary blends, and citing third-party lab testing for its claims.

## What Is MicroVitamin?

The brand was developed with input from Brad Stanfield, positioned in its marketing as a response to common supplement-industry practices like underdosing active ingredients, using cheap inactive nutrient forms, or padding formulas with unnecessary fillers. MicroVitamin says its formulas are third-party tested for purity and label accuracy through Labdoor.

## Product Lineup

### MicroVitamin (capsules)
A daily multivitamin combining 25 nutrients in what the brand describes as bioavailable, active forms — including methylated B vitamins, algae-derived vitamin D3, vitamin K2, magnesium taurate, and chelated minerals. Taken as 5 capsules per day. **Price:** $65 one-time ($58.50 on subscription).

### MicroVitamin+ Powder
An enhanced powder version of the formula with added collagen, creatine, fiber, and taurine. **Price:** $100 one-time ($90 on subscription).

**[→ Compare capsule vs. powder pricing](https://microvitamin.com)**

### Sleep
A separate melatonin and magnesium formula, sold independently from the core multivitamin line. **Price:** $40 one-time ($36 on subscription).

## Things to Know Before Buying

- **"Evidence-based" and "no fillers" are brand positioning claims** — worth checking the actual supplement facts panel (available on the product pages) rather than taking marketing copy at face value, as is true for any supplement brand.
- **Multivitamins are meant to fill dietary gaps, not replace a balanced diet** — the brand itself states that diet, exercise, and sleep should come first, which is a reasonable and honest framing worth taking at face value.
- **Subscription pricing requires recurring billing** — check the cancellation process before subscribing if you're not sure you'll want to continue.
- Third-party testing claims (Labdoor) are worth verifying directly if that's a deciding factor for you, since testing partnerships and scopes can change over time.
