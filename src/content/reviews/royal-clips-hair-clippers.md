## Pros

1. **Genuinely strong motor specs for the category** — 7,000-7,200 RPM brushless motors are a real step up from typical home clipper sets, and reviewers consistently back that up, describing them as powerful and comfortable to hold.
2. **Cordless convenience with a real-world 3-hour runtime**, enough for multiple haircuts per charge without being tethered to an outlet.
3. **The zero-gappable trimmer blade is built for close detail work** — hairlines, beard edges, and lining, which is exactly where cheap clippers usually fall short.
4. **A large, consistent review record** — 219+ on-site reviews averaging 4.9/5, plus an independently reported 4.4/5 on a separate review platform, is a reasonably strong signal across two different sources.
5. **Everything you need is in the box** — limit combs, oil, brushes, and chargers, so you're not immediately shopping for accessories after unboxing.
6. **Tiered bundles avoid overpaying** — you can start with the base Fade Kit and add charging bases or spare blades later rather than being forced into the priciest option.

![Individual Royal Clips tools — clipper, trimmer, and shaver](/images/blog/royalclips/individual-tools.jpg)

## Cons

1. **This is a premium purchase, not a budget one.** At $219.95 and up, it costs several times more than a basic home clipper set, which only makes sense if you're cutting hair regularly enough to justify it.
2. **Tools alone don't guarantee a barbershop fade.** Fading is a technique — blending, angle control, tension — that takes practice regardless of how good the equipment is; expect a learning curve even with professional-grade RPM behind you.
3. **The foil shaver may go unused** if your main goal is haircuts rather than close shaving or lining, making the standalone clipper (sold separately) worth considering instead of the full bundle.

## How It Compares

Against basic drugstore clipper sets, Royal Clips' brushless motors and cordless runtime are a genuine step up in build quality — the tradeoff is a price several times higher. Against true barbershop-grade equipment (the kind professional barbers buy for daily commercial use), Royal Clips positions itself as the serious-home-user tier: strong enough for frequent personal or family use, without the price tag or durability requirements of full-time commercial gear.

## Final Verdict: Is Royal Clips Worth Buying?

Royal Clips backs up its professional-grade positioning with real motor specs and a genuinely strong review record across two separate sources. It's a solid investment if you (or your household) cut hair often enough that the price premium pays for itself in skipped barbershop visits — just go in knowing that even great tools won't replace the practice it takes to nail a clean fade.
