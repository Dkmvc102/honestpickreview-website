## Overview

MicroVitamin markets itself around transparency in the supplement industry — publishing exact ingredient forms and dosages rather than using proprietary blends, and citing third-party lab testing for its claims. The brand was developed with input from Brad Stanfield.

## Product Lineup

### MicroVitamin (capsules)
A daily multivitamin combining 25 nutrients described as bioavailable, active forms. Taken as 5 capsules per day. **Price:** $65 one-time ($58.50 on subscription).

### MicroVitamin+ Powder
An enhanced powder version with added collagen, creatine, fiber, and taurine. **Price:** $100 one-time ($90 on subscription).

### Sleep
A separate melatonin and magnesium formula sold independently. **Price:** $40 one-time ($36 on subscription).

## Who It's Best For

Supplement shoppers who specifically want full-dose, transparently-labeled ingredients and are willing to pay more for that over a cheaper filler-heavy multivitamin.

## Things to Know Before Buying

- "Evidence-based" and "no fillers" are brand positioning claims — check the actual supplement facts panel rather than taking marketing copy at face value.
- The brand itself states diet, exercise, and sleep should come first, which is a reasonable framing for any multivitamin.
- Confirm the subscription cancellation process before subscribing if you're not sure you'll continue.

## Our Verdict

MicroVitamin is a solid pick for buyers who prioritize ingredient transparency and full-strength dosing over price. Budget-focused shoppers may find the per-serving cost hard to justify compared to standard drugstore options.
