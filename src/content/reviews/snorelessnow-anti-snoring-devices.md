## Cons

1. **"FDA-cleared" specifically means 510(k) clearance** — the device was cleared as substantially equivalent to an existing device on the market, which is a real regulatory process but distinct from the FDA independently verifying SnoreLessNow's specific effectiveness claims.
2. **Some customers have reported friction around refund requests**, including confusion about what's actually covered by the money-back guarantee versus shipping/handling costs — read the specific refund terms carefully before assuming a fully no-questions-asked process.
3. **Buying the full bundle to address multiple potential causes** adds up in cost compared to trying one product at a time, though the bundle discount does soften that.

## How It Compares

Against a single generic anti-snoring mouthpiece, SnoreLessNow's cause-based product split is more thoughtful, letting you target the pillow or nasal dilator first if you suspect a milder cause before committing to the pricier mouth guard. Against a sleep-apnea-specific medical device (which requires a prescription and professional fitting), SnoreLessNow's products are positioned for milder, non-apnea snoring — not a substitute if you actually have diagnosed or suspected sleep apnea.
