## Who It's Best For

Home cooks who make pizza regularly and want noticeably better crust than a stone delivers, and who don't mind the extra weight and preheat time that comes with steel.

## Things to Know Before Buying

- Preheat time matters — steel needs roughly 45-60 minutes in a hot oven to build up enough stored heat for its full crust-crisping effect.
- The weight (several pounds of solid steel) makes it less convenient to move or store than a lighter stone or aluminum pan.
- Compare the $129 price against your actual pizza-making frequency — occasional pizza makers may not see enough use to justify the cost over a cheaper stone.

## Our Verdict

Baking Steel is a strong pick for home cooks serious about pizza night, delivering a meaningfully crisper crust and faster bake than typical stones. Casual or infrequent pizza makers may get adequate results from a cheaper alternative.
