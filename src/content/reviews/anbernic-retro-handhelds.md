## What Is Anbernic?

Anbernic makes retro gaming handhelds spanning budget clamshell/flip devices built for straightforward emulation up to higher-end Android and Linux dual-boot models with modern smartphone-grade chipsets. The brand has been a consistent presence in the retro handheld category for years, with new models released regularly.

## Pros

1. **A genuinely wide range spanning budget to flagship**, so you can pick a device that matches your actual use case (light retro emulation vs. a fuller Android gaming handheld experience).
2. **Consistent, credible independent tech coverage.** Established retro-gaming-focused reviewers — Retro Game Corps, ETA PRIME, Retro Dodo, and Wulff Den — regularly review and generally recommend specific Anbernic models, which is a meaningful signal from a niche with genuinely knowledgeable reviewers.
3. **Modern specs on higher-end models** — WiFi 6, Bluetooth 5.2, 120Hz displays, and fast charging bring the hardware up to a genuinely current standard, not just repackaged old tech.
4. **Dual-boot flexibility** on select models (Android + Linux) extends usefulness beyond pure retro emulation into general Android app use.
5. **A large volume of detailed on-site reviews** (4,700+), with specific comments about build quality and performance rather than generic five-star praise.

## Cons

1. **Pricing transparency could be better** — most models don't list price directly on the homepage, requiring a click-through to see actual cost.
2. **Component and durability reports vary by specific model and generation.** Some user reports mention button or battery charging issues, particularly when not using the included cable — worth checking recent reviews for the exact model you're considering rather than assuming consistent quality across the whole lineup.
3. **Direct international ordering carries the usual considerations of a China-based DTC electronics brand** — shipping times and support responsiveness for buyers outside the brand's core markets can be a real factor, so factor that into your decision if it matters to you.
