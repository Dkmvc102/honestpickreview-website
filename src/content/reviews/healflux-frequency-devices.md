## Overview

Healflux sells a range of wellness gadgets built around "frequency, vibration & light" concepts — including a handheld frequency device, LED light therapy panels, plasma-based devices, and "quantum" jewelry.

**We want to be direct about this one:** claims involving "frequency healing," "scalar energy," and "quantum" wellness effects are not supported by mainstream scientific evidence, and Healflux's own site includes a disclaimer stating its products are not medical devices and are not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease. We're covering it here because it appeared in our research list, but we want that context front and center rather than buried at the bottom.

## Things to Know Before Buying

- **Evaluate marketing language critically.** Terms like "frequency," "vibration," "scalar," and "quantum" are common in wellness marketing but don't correspond to established, measurable mechanisms in mainstream medical science for consumer devices like these.
- **The brand's own "not a medical device" disclaimer is a meaningful signal** — it means Healflux itself isn't making formal medical efficacy claims, legally speaking, even where the surrounding marketing language may suggest wellness benefits.
- **If you're addressing an actual health condition, see a doctor** rather than relying on unproven wellness devices as a substitute for evidence-based care.
- The light therapy panel is the one product type in this lineup with at least some legitimate research precedent for light therapy generally — though that doesn't confirm this specific product matches the parameters used in that research.

## Our Verdict

We're rating this one more cautiously than other products we cover. If you're curious to experiment with frequency/vibration wellness products for personal interest, that's your call to make — but go in with clear eyes that these claims aren't scientifically established, and don't substitute any of these devices for medical care.
