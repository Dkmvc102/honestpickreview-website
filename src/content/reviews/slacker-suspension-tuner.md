If you've ever tried to set your suspension sag alone — balancing the bike, holding a tape measure, and squinting at an o-ring mark all at once — you already know why Slacker exists. It's a digital gauge built around a single complaint: sag measurement shouldn't require a second person.

## What Is Slacker?

Slacker makes the **Digital Suspension Tuner**, a patented device that clips to your bike's axle with a strong magnet and a retractable cable, then streams a live sag reading to a wireless remote or the free Slacker Virtual Remote app on your phone. Instead of eyeballing where an o-ring sits against a ruler while someone holds the bike upright, you get a digital number in real time, by yourself.

## How It Works & What's Included

The core unit mounts via a magnetic base to the rear axle and connects through a retractable cable to a universal clip on the fork or shock. As you compress the suspension, it tracks the travel and displays sag on the wireless remote or the companion app, which also logs your settings over time — useful if you're chasing a specific setup across several track days or trail rides.

It ships with the attachments needed for dirt, street, and adventure bikes to measure both shock and fork sag. Mountain bikers need the optional Virtual Fender Mount for rear-suspension readings, sold separately. It runs on 2 AAA batteries, which aren't included in the box.

## Pros

1. **Genuinely solo operation** — the magnet-and-cable design is the whole point, and it removes the single biggest friction point of traditional sag setup: needing someone else to hold the bike and read the mark.
2. **Digital precision over guesswork.** A tape measure and an o-ring depend on how carefully you read the mark; a digital readout doesn't have that ambiguity.
3. **The app builds a tuning log over time**, which matters if you're adjusting settings across multiple rides or track sessions and want to compare against a past baseline rather than starting from scratch each time.
4. **Broad compatibility** across dirt bikes, street bikes, and adventure bikes out of the box, with an MTB path available via the optional mount.
5. **30-day money-back guarantee** takes some of the risk out of trying a tool most riders haven't used before.
6. **Verified outside the brand's own site** — Full Throttle and Pete Organics, both independent motorcycle/MTB outlets, tested and recommended it rather than only running brand-supplied testimonials.

![Slacker Virtual Remote app displaying live sag readings](/images/blog/slacker/app-display.jpg)

## Cons

1. **It measures accurately, but you still have to mount and stand consistently.** The tool removes reading error, not technique error — sloppy mounting or inconsistent body position between measurements will still throw off results.
2. **Sag is one variable, not the whole tune.** Independent reviewers are consistent on this: rebound, compression damping, and spring rate still need separate attention, and this tool doesn't touch any of them.
3. **A couple of extra costs aren't in the base price** — batteries aren't included, and mountain bikers need the separately sold Virtual Fender Mount for rear readings.

## How It Compares

Against the traditional o-ring-and-tape-measure method, Slacker trades a roughly $150-225 upfront cost for speed, repeatability, and not needing a second person — a fair trade for anyone who tunes more than once or twice a season. Against a full professional suspension shop tune, Slacker isn't a replacement — it nails down one input (sag) accurately, while a suspension specialist still has the edge on dialing in rebound and compression for your specific riding style.

## Final Verdict: Is Slacker Worth Buying?

Slacker solves a specific, real annoyance — solo, accurate sag measurement — and backs it up with both a strong on-site review volume and independent editorial coverage reaching the same conclusion. It's most worth it for riders who adjust suspension regularly (track days, frequent trail changes, dialing in a new bike); if you set sag once a season and leave it, the traditional method may be precision enough. For everyone else, the 30-day guarantee makes it a low-risk way to find out.
