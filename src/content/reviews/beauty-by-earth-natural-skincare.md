## Pros

1. **Unusually deep review counts per individual product** — 2,543 reviews on the flagship self-tanner alone is a substantial, specific sample rarely seen even among established beauty brands.
2. **A genuinely coherent, multi-category product range** rather than a single hero product padded out with filler SKUs — self-tanning, skincare, and body/hair care all get real product development attention.
3. **Specific, checkable clean-beauty claims** — mineral sunscreen, hyaluronic acid and vitamin C serums are concrete formulation choices, not just vague "natural" marketing language.
4. **A generous 60-day money-back guarantee** gives real room to test whether a product (especially self-tanner, where individual skin reaction varies) works for you specifically.
5. **Independent data is directionally consistent** with on-site sentiment — a 4.2/5 rating across 65 reviews on a separate aggregator supports rather than contradicts the large on-site review base.

## Cons

1. **Trustpilot's sample for this brand is too small to be meaningful on its own** — just 2 reviews, which is a real limitation compared to brands where we found large, independently verified samples.
2. **Self-tanner application technique matters a lot**, and dissatisfaction with any self-tanning brand often traces back to application error (uneven exfoliation, missed spots) rather than the formula itself — worth following application best practices regardless of brand.
3. **"Natural" claims deserve a look at the actual ingredient list** for your specific product and any personal sensitivities, rather than assuming the category label covers every formulation equally.

## What Customers Are Saying

Beauty by Earth's most compelling evidence is the sheer depth of per-product review data — 2,543 reviews on the flagship self-tanner, with review counts in the hundreds across most of the rest of the catalog, suggesting genuine, sustained product usage rather than a burst of initial launch reviews. A separate independent review aggregator shows a corroborating **4.2/5 rating across 65 reviews**, broadly consistent with the on-site sentiment. Trustpilot specifically has too small a sample (2 reviews) to add much independent confidence on its own, which we think is fair to flag directly rather than either ignore or overstate. The brand's claimed "2 Million+ Happy Customers" figure, while self-reported, is at least directionally plausible given the scale of individual product review counts we could independently observe.

## How It Compares

Against mass-market drugstore self-tanners and skincare, Beauty by Earth's natural/mineral-focused formulations and specific ingredient claims (hyaluronic acid, vitamin C, mineral SPF) position it as a mid-market step up for buyers who prioritize ingredient transparency. Against premium clean-beauty brands charging significantly more, Beauty by Earth's pricing ($11.99-$57.98) sits at a more accessible tier while still offering the natural-ingredient positioning that premium brands market around.

## Final Verdict: Is Beauty by Earth Worth Buying?

Beauty by Earth backs up its clean-beauty positioning with unusually deep, product-specific review volume and a generous return window, making it a reasonably low-risk way to try natural-ingredient self-tanning and skincare. It's a solid mid-market choice if ingredient-conscious beauty products matter to you — just apply standard self-tanner technique best practices, and check the specific ingredient list against any personal sensitivities before ordering.
