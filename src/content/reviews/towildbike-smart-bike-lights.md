## Pros

1. **Anti-glare cutoff beam technology is a genuine, meaningful engineering feature**, not just marketing language — it directly addresses a real safety and courtesy problem with cheaper, undirected bike lights.
2. **Dual-beam (focus/flood) modes on select models** add practical versatility, letting one light adapt to both open-road and technical trail conditions.
3. **A wide lumen range** across the lineup means you can match light output to your actual riding conditions rather than over- or under-buying.
4. **GoPro-compatible mounting** on higher-end models adds flexibility for riders who also want to mount an action camera.
5. **Frequent, substantial promotional pricing** (25-37% off list price) makes the higher-lumen models considerably more accessible.

## Cons

1. **The headline 4.89-star rating is self-hosted**, not independently verified on a platform like Trustpilot that we could cross-check — worth knowing as you weigh how much to rely on that specific number, even though a 1,550-review volume itself suggests real usage.
2. **Battery runtime varies significantly by brightness setting.** Check the specific runtime spec at your intended usage brightness, not just the peak-lumen headline number, when comparing models.
3. **Waterproofing rigor should be verified for your conditions.** "Waterproof" claims vary in actual testing standards across the bike light category broadly — check the specific IP rating if you ride in consistently wet conditions.
