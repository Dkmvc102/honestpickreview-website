## What Is Porch Potty?

Porch Potty makes real-grass dog potty systems designed for balconies, patios, and indoor use, positioned as a more dog-friendly alternative to synthetic pee pads. The core design uses a self-draining tray under a grass surface (synthetic or live sod), with higher-tier models adding pop-up sprinklers for automatic rinsing.

## Pros

1. **Real grass, not a synthetic pad**, which many dogs take to more naturally — the brand specifically markets the "scent-trigger" effect of grass on a dog's natural instincts.
2. **Five sizes cover a genuinely wide range of living situations**, from a small apartment balcony (Mini) to a full yard-replacement setup (Mega).
3. **Self-draining design solves the actual mess problem.** A DIY grass pad without drainage becomes unusable fast; Porch Potty's tray system is built specifically to manage that.
4. **Auto-rinse sprinklers on Premium and Mega models** remove a genuine chore — you're not manually hosing down the grass after every use.
5. **A large, if platform-variable, review base.** Between Sitejabber (3.5/5, 40 reviews), RealReviews (4.1/5, 16 reviews), and a third-party platform showing 4.7/5 across more than 8,000 reviews, there's a meaningful volume of independent feedback to draw on.

## Cons

1. **This is a real investment**, not an impulse buy — $589.95 for the Mega model is a significant cost compared to disposable pads or a DIY tray setup.
2. **Review consistency varies by platform.** The spread from 3.5/5 to 4.7/5 across different review sites suggests experience varies somewhat by customer, so it's worth reading a range of reviews rather than anchoring on the single highest score.
3. **Ongoing costs apply with live sod.** If you choose real sod over reusable synthetic grass, budget for the recurring subscription cost, not just the one-time system purchase.

Porch Potty's review picture is genuinely multi-source, which we think is worth presenting honestly rather than cherry-picking the best number. **Sitejabber** shows 3.5/5 from 40 reviews, with customer service coming up as a frequent theme in feedback. **RealReviews.io** shows a stronger 4.1/5 from 16 reviews. Taken together, the larger-sample sources skew clearly positive, while the smaller Sitejabber sample is more middling — a reasonable takeaway is that most customers are satisfied, with a meaningful minority having a rockier experience worth being aware of.

## How It Compares

Against disposable pee pads, Porch Potty trades a much higher upfront cost for a reusable, more natural surface that many dogs prefer and that doesn't generate ongoing pad waste. Against building your own DIY grass box, Porch Potty's engineered drainage system and optional auto-rinse sprinklers solve the maintenance problems that make most DIY setups short-lived.

## Final Verdict: Is Porch Potty Worth Buying?

Porch Potty backs up its premium positioning with a genuinely large body of customer feedback, even if the exact rating varies somewhat by platform. It's a strong option for apartment dwellers, owners of dogs with mobility issues, or anyone house-training a puppy who wants a real-grass, low-maintenance solution — just budget for the higher upfront cost and, if you go the live-sod route, the ongoing subscription.
