If getting your kids (or yourself) to actually take a daily vitamin consistently feels like a losing battle — spat-out pills, forgotten capsules, the same half-full bottle sitting in the cupboard for months — that's the exact problem NUTRIQ is built around. This review looks at whether their vitamin gummies actually solve that problem, and whether the brand itself is worth trusting with your order.

## What Is NUTRIQ?

NUTRIQ is a Germany-based supplement brand that makes vitamin gummies — chewable, candy-like vitamins — for both kids and adults. The pitch is straightforward: instead of one generic multivitamin, NUTRIQ splits its lineup into focused formulas (immune support, metabolic support, calcium & vitamin D3, hair & beauty) plus dedicated children's products, developed with input from German pediatricians according to the brand. The company positions itself as "smart supplements for the whole family" — highly dosed, sensibly combined, doctor-developed.

This review is based on the product information published on NUTRIQ's official site, plus independent customer feedback found on Trustpilot and Trustedshops (details below) — not on our own lab testing of the ingredients.

## Key Ingredients & What They're Meant to Do

Across the range, NUTRIQ's formulas are built to be vegan, gluten-free, non-GMO, and sugar-free — a combination that rules out several common allergens and dietary blockers in one go. The specific active ingredients vary by product:

- **Kids Multivitamin & Omega-3** combines a multivitamin blend with Omega-3 fatty acids, aimed at covering everyday nutritional gaps in a child's diet.
- **Immune Booster** is formulated around immune-support nutrients (the brand doesn't publish the full breakdown on its homepage — check the individual product page for the exact vitamin/mineral list before buying).
- **Metabolic Booster** targets metabolic support for adults.
- **Kids Calcium & Vitamin D3** focuses on the two nutrients most associated with bone development and mobility in growing children.
- **Hair Beauty** is an adult formula aimed at hair and skin support.

As with any supplement brand, these are the manufacturer's stated purposes for each ingredient blend, not independently verified clinical outcomes — treat the "booster" naming as marketing framing for what the nutrients are generally associated with, not a guarantee of a specific effect.

## Pros

1. **Genuinely allergen-conscious formulation.** Vegan, gluten-free, non-GMO, and sugar-free isn't just a buzzword combo here — it directly addresses several common reasons families avoid gummy vitamins (gelatin, sugar content, GMO concerns).
2. **Split, focused formulas instead of one catch-all gummy.** Buying Immune Booster or Hair Beauty separately means you're not paying for a dozen ingredients you don't need, and can combine only the formulas relevant to you.
3. **Dedicated kids' line with pediatrician input claimed.** Separate dosing and formulation for children is more appropriate than adults and kids sharing the same gummy at different serving sizes.
4. **Format solves a real adherence problem.** For kids (and plenty of adults) who don't swallow pills easily, a gummy that's actually pleasant to take is more likely to be taken consistently than a capsule that gets skipped.
5. **Kids Complete bundle simplifies buying for parents** who want multiple formulas without ordering each one separately.

## Cons

1. **Shipping and service complaints show up repeatedly in independent reviews.** This is the biggest flag from our research — see the Trustpilot/Trustedshops section below.
2. **Popular items go out of stock.** Kids Multivitamin & Omega-3 and the Kids Complete bundle were both listed as unavailable at the time of writing, which is frustrating if you're trying to start a consistent routine.
3. **Cost per serving is higher than a basic pharmacy multivitamin**, which is typical for gummy-format supplements generally, not unique to NUTRIQ, but worth factoring into your budget if you're supplementing multiple family members.

## What Customers Are Saying

We looked at independent review platforms rather than only relying on testimonials NUTRIQ features on its own site, since self-selected on-site reviews tend to skew positive.

**Trustpilot** shows a notably mixed picture for nutriq.health — ratings found in our research ranged from roughly **2.4 to 2.9 out of 5**, based on a small number of reviews (under 10 at the time of writing, so treat this as a limited but real signal, not a definitive verdict). The pattern is polarized: several 5-star reviews alongside a larger share of 1-star reviews, with few reviews in the middle.

**Trustedshops** reviews (more commonly used for German e-commerce) echo a similar theme: multiple customers reported deliveries arriving significantly later than the "2-3 days" shipping estimate shown at checkout — with more than one review citing wait times over a month. On the positive side, several customers noted that once they contacted support directly, their issue was resolved quickly and the experience "pleasantly surprised" them — suggesting the fulfillment/shipping process, not the customer service team's willingness to help, is the weaker link.

Importantly, the complaints we found center on **order fulfillment and delivery timing**, not on the gummies causing problems or not working as expected. We didn't find a pattern of product-quality or safety complaints in the reviews we reviewed.

*A note relevant to German and EU shoppers specifically: dietary supplement advertising in Germany is regulated under the HWG (Heilmittelwerbegesetz), which restricts health claims for products like these. We've deliberately avoided repeating specific medical or therapeutic claims in this review beyond what NUTRIQ states about general nutritional support, and you should treat any health-outcome claims — from NUTRIQ or anyone else — with appropriate skepticism.*

## How It Compares

Against a brand like [MicroVitamin](/reviews/health-supplements/microvitamin-multivitamin/), which competes on ingredient-dose transparency rather than format, NUTRIQ's main differentiator is the gummy format itself and the dedicated kids' line — MicroVitamin doesn't currently offer a children's product. If your priority is a format your kids will actually take without a fight, NUTRIQ's approach is more purpose-built for that than a capsule-based competitor; if your priority is maximum ingredient transparency and you don't need a kids' formula, it's worth comparing both product labels side by side before deciding.

## Final Verdict: Is NUTRIQ Worth Buying?

NUTRIQ's product concept is solid — allergen-conscious, focused formulas in a format that solves a real problem for families who struggle with pill-based vitamins. The ingredient positioning holds up reasonably well against competitors. Where NUTRIQ loses points is fulfillment: the shipping delay complaints on Trustpilot and Trustedshops are frequent enough, and specific enough (multiple people citing over a month vs. an advertised 2-3 days), that we'd flag it clearly rather than bury it in a "things to know" footnote.

**Our take:** if you're not in a rush and mainly care about the product itself, NUTRIQ is a reasonable pick, particularly for the kids' formulas where few direct competitors exist. If you need the vitamins quickly (starting a trip, running out of an existing supply), factor in the realistic possibility of a longer wait than the checkout page suggests, or consider ordering via NUTRIQ's Amazon.de listing instead, where Amazon's own shipping and returns process applies.
