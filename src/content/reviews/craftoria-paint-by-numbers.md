## Pros

1. **A genuinely large catalog** (2,000+ designs) means you're unlikely to run out of options that match your taste, unlike smaller competitors with a handful of stock designs.
2. **The custom photo option is a real differentiator** — turning a personal photo (a pet, a family member, a favorite landscape) into a paint-by-numbers project is more meaningful than a generic stock design.
3. **Genuinely beginner-friendly by design** — the numbered format removes the technical skill barrier that keeps a lot of people from trying painting as a hobby at all.
4. **Practical format flexibility** — framed vs. unframed, and mini kits for smaller projects, accommodate different display plans and time commitments.
5. **Value-friendly promotions** — a recurring "buy 2, get 1 free" bundle and loyalty rewards program improve the economics if you plan to make this an ongoing hobby.

## Cons

1. **We want to be upfront about a research limitation here.** Craftoria's own site cites "120,000+ happy painters," but we couldn't find this figure independently verified on a third-party review platform like Trustpilot. That doesn't mean it's inaccurate, just that we're presenting it as a brand-reported claim rather than something we could independently confirm — a more limited signal than brands where we found matching third-party review data.
2. **Custom kit quality is genuinely dependent on your source photo.** A blurry, low-contrast, or low-resolution photo will produce a less satisfying finished kit no matter how good Craftoria's conversion process is — choose your source image carefully.
3. **The hobby format itself has a ceiling.** Paint-by-numbers is intentionally more structured and less artistically flexible than freeform painting — a fair tradeoff for approachability, but worth knowing if you're hoping to develop more advanced painting technique over time.
