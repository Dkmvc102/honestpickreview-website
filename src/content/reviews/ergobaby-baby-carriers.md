## Cons

1. **US shipping and customer service complaints are a real, documented pattern** — delays, unclear shipment communication, and non-delivery reports appear across multiple US reviews.
2. **Return policy friction is specifically cited** by some reviewers as a frustrating part of the post-purchase process.
3. **The regional rating gap (3.2/5 US vs. 4-4.5/5 EU) suggests logistics/support, not the carrier itself, is the primary weak point** — worth factoring in heavily based on where you're ordering from.

## What Customers Are Saying

Ergobaby's review picture varies meaningfully by region — the US Trustpilot page sits at **3.2/5 across 222 reviews**, while European pages run notably higher (4-4.5/5 in France and Germany). Across both regions, reviewers consistently praise the actual carrier quality and comfort, with the negative reviews concentrated specifically on shipping delays, unclear communication about shipment status, and return-policy friction — a logistics and support problem more than a hardware problem. This pattern is worth taking seriously if you're ordering from the US specifically, while it appears to be less of a factor for European customers based on the regional data.

## How It Compares

Against newer, unproven baby carrier brands, Ergobaby's long-established reputation and extensive independent baby-gear testing provide meaningfully more confidence in the actual hardware and hip-healthy design engineering. Against carriers with less robust safety/ergonomic engineering credentials, Ergobaby's specific focus on hip-healthy positioning is a genuine differentiator for a product category where that consideration matters for infant development — the tradeoff being real, region-dependent shipping and service friction to plan around.

## Final Verdict: Is Ergobaby Worth Buying?

Ergobaby delivers on its core promise — genuinely ergonomic, hip-healthy carrier design backed by a long, established reputation and consistent product-quality praise across regions. It's a solid choice for parents prioritizing carrier engineering, with the caveat that US shoppers specifically should order with extra lead time given the documented shipping and customer service friction reflected in the lower US Trustpilot score.
