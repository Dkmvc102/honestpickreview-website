Both drills cover the same core use case — general household drilling and driving — but differ in motor technology and price point.

## Quick Comparison

| | TorquePro 20V | ForgeMax 18V Brushless |
|---|---|---|
| Price range | $60–$90 | $90–$130 |
| Motor | Brushed | Brushless |
| Chuck | 1/2" keyless | 1/2" all-metal keyless |
| Clutch positions | 21 + drill | Standard clutch |
| Best for | Occasional DIY use | Frequent DIY / light trade use |

## Where TorquePro Wins

The TorquePro is the better pick if you only reach for a drill a few times a month — its lower price makes it easy to justify, and the 21-position clutch gives fine control for driving screws without stripping them.

## Where ForgeMax Wins

The ForgeMax's brushless motor means more efficient power delivery, a longer motor lifespan, and typically more runtime per battery charge. For anyone drilling and driving several times a week, that efficiency difference adds up over the tool's lifetime.

## Our Recommendation

If budget is the primary concern and use is occasional, go with the **TorquePro 20V**. If you'll be using the drill regularly over several years, the **ForgeMax 18V Brushless** is worth the extra upfront cost.
